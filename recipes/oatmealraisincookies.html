Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Description

Oatmeal raisin cookies are a nice sweet treat to cap off a night. This recipe makes four dozen soft and chewy oatmeal raisin cookies. The use of rolled oats is ideal, but old-fashioned oats may also be used. It is advised to avoid using steel cut oats or instant oats. Store the prepared cookies at room temperature and they should be good-as-new for up to a week.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 3/4 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup raisins

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Line two cookie sheets with parchment paper or silicone liners.
  2. Beat butter, white sugar, and brown sugar in a large bowl until smooth and creamy. Beat in eggs and vanilla until fluffy.
  3. Stir together flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Gradually beat into the butter mixture. Stir in oats and raisins. Drop teaspoonfuls of batter onto prepared cookie sheets.
  4. Bake in the oven until golden brown, 8 to 10 minutes, switching racks halfwy through. Remove from the oven and let sit on the cookie sheet for 1 to 2 minutes before transferring cookies to wire rack to cool.
